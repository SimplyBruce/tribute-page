O'Shea Jackson "Ice Cube"

O'Shea Jackson, better known by his stage name Ice Cube, is an American rapper, actor, and filmmaker.

Here's a time line of "Ice Cube's" life

  • 1969 Born in Los Angeles, California, U.S.
  • 1986 Formed his first rap group "C.I.A."
  • 1987 Formed a pioneering gangsta rap group, "N.W.A" with Eazy-E and Dr. Dre.
  • 1989 Left "N.W.A" via monetary dispute with its management by Eazy-E and by Jerry Heller. Cube teamed with New York artists and launched a solo rap career
  • 1991 He entered cinema by playing "Doughboy" in director John Singleton's feature debut "Boyz n the Hood", namesake of an 1987 rap song that Cube wrote.
  • 1995 Cube cowrote and starred in the comedy film "Friday", "coarse and ribald", premising Friday sequels, reshaping his persona into friendly movie star.
  • 2020 By 2020, his acting roles include about 40 films, like the 1999 war comedy "Three Kings", family comedies like the "Barbershop" series begun in 2002, and the "Ride Along" comedies near 2015. He was an executive producer of some of these, and of the 2015 biopic "Straight Outta Compton", on N.W.A's story.
